Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 11, 2026 · Pawffles

Short version: Pawffles is a memory game with a weekly leaderboard. We don't ask for your email, phone, name, or location. We share the minimum needed: your nickname and weekly score for the ranking, an anonymous device advertising identifier for Google AdMob to show ads, and anonymous usage events (which screens you tap) so we can improve the app. You can delete your data from inside the app at any time.

1. Who we are

Pawffles is an independent mobile game published by Morsa Software. You can reach us at morsasoftware@gmail.com.

2. What we collect

  • An anonymous user ID — a random identifier generated by our backend (Supabase). It cannot be linked to you personally.
  • The nickname you choose — shown in the weekly leaderboard. You can pick anything; please do not use your real name, email, or phone number.
  • Your weekly score — the total points you earned in the current week's daily challenges, used to compute your position in the ranking.
  • Advertising identifier (Google Advertising ID) — an anonymous device-level ID used by Google AdMob to display ads and prevent fraud. You can reset or limit it from Android (Settings → Google → Ads).
  • Anonymous usage events — which screens you open and which buttons you tap inside the app. PostHog processes these so we can understand how Pawffles is used and decide what to improve. Events are tied to an anonymous identifier separate from the leaderboard one.

3. What we do NOT collect

  • No email, phone number, real name, or contact list.
  • No location, GPS, or IP-based geolocation.
  • No microphone, camera, photos, or files.
  • No sensitive data (health, orientation, religion, etc.).
  • We do not sell your data to brokers or any third party.

4. Data stored only on your device

Your level progress, stars, boosters, daily best scores, and audio settings are stored locally on your device using Android's standard local storage. They never leave your phone unless you reinstall or transfer the app.

5. How we use your data

  • The anonymous user ID, nickname, and weekly score are used to display the weekly leaderboard inside the app.
  • The advertising identifier is used by Google AdMob to show ads inside the app and prevent fraud/abuse.
  • Anonymous usage events are used through PostHog to understand which parts of the app are most used and decide what to improve or remove.

We do not sell your data. We do not share it with brokers or third parties beyond the sub-processors described in the next section.

6. Who processes your data

We work with the following sub-processors:

  • Supabase — backend for the weekly leaderboard and the anonymous session. Policy: supabase.com/privacy.
  • Google AdMob — in-app advertising network. Policy: policies.google.com/privacy.
  • PostHog (PostHog Cloud EU) — anonymous usage analytics, hosted in the European Union. Policy: posthog.com/privacy.

7. How long we keep your data

Weekly score rows are kept indefinitely so historical leaderboards remain consistent. You can delete your data at any time (see next section).

8. Deleting your data

Open the app, tap the gear icon on the level select screen, then tap "Delete my data". This will:

  • Erase your local progress, nickname, and game state.
  • Delete your leaderboard rows from our backend.
  • Delete the nickname reservation.
  • Sign you out and start a fresh anonymous session.

If for any reason the in-app flow doesn't work, email us at morsasoftware@gmail.com and we will remove your data manually.

9. Minimum age and children's privacy

Pawffles is intended for users 13 years of age or older. It is not designed for, or directed at, children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under that age. If you are a parent or guardian and believe a child under 13 is using the app or has provided personal information through a nickname, contact us at morsasoftware@gmail.com and we will delete that information promptly.

10. Security

Data sent between the app and our backend uses HTTPS encryption. Supabase enforces row-level security so a user can only modify their own rows. No system is perfectly secure, but the data we hold is minimal and not personally identifying on its own.

11. Your rights

You can request access to or deletion of the data tied to your anonymous ID at any time. The fastest way is the in-app deletion flow; you can also email us. Depending on your jurisdiction (e.g. GDPR in the EU, LGPD in Brazil), you may have additional rights — contact us to exercise them.

12. Changes to this policy

If we make material changes, we will update the effective date at the top and, if appropriate, notify users in the app. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance.

13. Contact

Questions, requests, or concerns: morsasoftware@gmail.com.
